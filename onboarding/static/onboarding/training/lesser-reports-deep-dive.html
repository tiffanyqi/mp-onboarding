Deep Dive into Lesser Used Reports

Formulas

Formulas allow you to stay out of excel and do arithmetic. This is useful for quickly calculating ratios and KPIs and seeing how those rates change over time

  • Allows you to perform arithmetical operations on any Mixpanel event or a subset of those events (Segmented by a property)
  • Allows you to stay out of excel and complete operations directly within the webapp
  • Great for calculating ratios, KPIS (Useful for quickly calculating rates and seeing how those rates change over time)
  • Similar to Segmentation, but not quite the same
  • How is this calculated?
    • High level - select 2 events and an operation (app frequency)
    • Based on formulas API
    • Pass on parameters to endpoint and it passes you back the value
  • Limitations:
    • Calculations based on event counts, no way to perform calculation on value of an event’s properties
    • Ability to compare different formulas, limited at 2 formulas- bookmark several different formula reports
  • 2% of users use this report - so users don’t have to export and use excel to do conversions

Insights

Insight is to people what segmentation is to events - gives you snapshot of all people properties allowing you to run distributions across all of your users and gauge things like average revenue or churn

  • Plotting out frequency of people properties across profiles
    • If you delete people profiles, their data will not exist in people, but it will still exist in events
    • Dates on Insights - just for property data, not for the time the event happened
  • Insight is to people what segmentation is to events
    • Gives you snapshot of all people properties, allowing you to run distributions across all of your users and gauge things like average revenue or churn
    • You select x and y axes and how you want to visualize data
  • Insight gives you a snapshot of all the people properties in every people profile at this current point in time
  • Good report: Venmo’s number of friends by transactions report, also revenue by age
  • General advice with insights is, it’s better to have a question first
  • It’s hard to come up with a graph that means something, but if it’s a relationship between two people properties - insights can do that
  • Technically an alpha product
  • Limitations:
    • Can only look at snapshot of all your people profiles
    • There isn’t a way to see how properties change over time
    • Y-axis in insight report is limited to top 6 values for strings
    • The limit goes up to top 14 values in the property is an integer
    • What about list properties or booleans? (Hint: No bueno)
    • If you’re plotting a number, it cannot have less than 12 buckets (E.g. If you had an app that was from 0 to 6 year olds, there would be half buckets)
    • That can have really weird consequences if you have purchases with 99 cents
    • Last bucket is 95th percentile (Looks way bigger)
